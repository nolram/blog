I finished 2025 having read 18 books, a reasonable number for a busy year. Writing down the ones that stuck with me has become a habit: it helps fix what I learned and serves as a reference when someone asks for recommendations.

## Highlights of the year

### Fiction

**_The Book of the New Sun_ — Gene Wolfe**
Wolfe is the kind of author who demands re-reading. Unreliable narrator, dense prose and a cosmology that reveals itself gradually. It took me three weeks to finish and I was left with the feeling of having understood 70% — which, for Wolfe, is probably a good rate.

**_Exhalation_ — Ted Chiang**
A collection of philosophical science fiction stories. "The Lifecycle of Software Objects" and "Anxiety Is the Dizziness of Freedom" are perfect. Chiang writes stories that seem simple and leave behind questions that are impossible to answer.

### Non-fiction

**_The Art of Doing Science and Engineering_ — Richard Hamming**
A transcript of a lecture series Hamming gave to young researchers at the Naval Postgraduate School. Each chapter is a career lesson disguised as a technical talk. The central question of the book — "what are the important problems in your field and why aren't you working on them?" — still unsettles me.

**_Antifragile_ — Nassim Nicholas Taleb**
I had already read _The Black Swan_ and _Fooled by Randomness_. This one closes the trilogy with the most useful concept: systems that benefit from volatility rather than merely surviving it. The application to software infrastructure is immediate.
