IC 434 is an emission nebula in the constellation Orion, known for the dark silhouette shaped like a horse's head — Barnard 33 — set against the reddish glow of ionized hydrogen. It sits roughly 1,500 light-years away and is one of the most photographed targets in the southern hemisphere's winter sky.

The challenge here is the sky. Porto Alegre has considerable light pollution, and Orion sits relatively low on the horizon during much of the capture window. The Optolong L-eXtreme filter is what made the image viable: it isolates the Hα and OIII bands, cutting out most of the artificial light continuum. The result is a real gain in contrast on the target, even without leaving the city.

The ASKAR FRA400 is compact and opens to f/5.6 natively — with the 0.7x focal reducer it goes down to f/3.9, which helps considerably with integration time. The ASI533MC Pro, being a cooled camera, keeps thermal noise under control even during long sessions.
